Help & Resources

If you or someone you love is struggling, there is help available.

Emergency & Crisis

  • Emergency: Call 911 if someone is in immediate danger.
  • Mental Health Crisis (U.S.): Call or text 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline.

Homelessness Support

  • Dial 211 (in many areas) to be connected with local shelters and housing programs.
  • Search online for “homeless shelter near me” to find local beds, daytime centers, and outreach teams.
  • Many churches and community centers offer food, showers, clothing, and basic supplies—ask what’s available in your area.

Addiction & Recovery

  • Look for local detox centers and inpatient/outpatient rehab programs.
  • Support groups like AA, NA, and other recovery meetings can be found by searching online in your city.
  • If you don’t know where to start, call a local clinic or hospital and ask for their addiction services or referrals.

Mental Health & Family Support

  • Many cities have low-cost or sliding-scale therapists and counseling centers.
  • Families of people struggling with addiction can look for support groups (like Al-Anon or other family support meetings).
  • If money is tight, ask clinics about free support groups, community mental health services, or online options.

Whether you are the one struggling, or you love someone who is, you are not alone. Help may not always be easy to find, but it exists. Keep asking, keep reaching out, and keep believing that things can change.

In Loving Memory of Delino Garcia

Delino Garcia was more than a name. He was a loving brother, a good son, a fun uncle, and a hardworking man who traveled the country behind the wheel of a big rig.

He loved football games, new cities, and the little things like collecting shot glasses from the places he visited. He loved hard, worked hard, and carried his family in his heart everywhere he went.

His Road, His Legacy

Family First: He was a protector and a symbol of strength for his loved ones.
On the Road: As an over-the-road truck driver, he saw more states and highways than most people ever will.
Heart of Gold: He helped others, gave what he could, and always looked out for his people.
Forever Loved: His family, friends, and community will carry his name, his laugh, and his spirit forever.

The Road to Home Foundation is a living tribute to Delino. The same highways he drove on are the roads where many people sleep, struggle, and search for hope. In his honor, we choose to show up for them. Every meal given, every resource shared, every person helped off the street — it’s part of his story now, too.

A Message to Delino

We miss you every day. You traveled across this country and now you’ve traveled somewhere our eyes can’t see yet. We carry you in our hearts, in our work, and in every life we touch through this foundation. This is for you, little brother. Always.
